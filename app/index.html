    Climate

    Environment and Climate Change Canada, Canadian Climate Normals 1981-2010, via the GeoMet API. Each place gets one home station: the nearest with a complete record, weighed on distance, elevation, and how many years the normal was actually computed from. Every place shows which station and how far off it is. Revelstoke's nearest published station is 43km away and 1,431m up a mountain, reporting 1,388cm of snow. That is the alpine snowpack, not the town. Nine places have no published normals nearby; theirs are computed from raw monthly observations and say so.

    Wildfire smoke

    ECCC FireWork RAQDPS-FW Cumulative Effects, the air quality model run with fires differenced against the run without, so it is smoke from fire only and not traffic or wood stoves. 2013-2024 mean, 10km grid. The obvious Environment Canada field, "days with smoke or haze", is negatively correlated with real wildfire smoke and is not used here.

    The rest

    Population, income, dwelling values and rent from the 2021 Census, at census subdivision level. Home value is what owners estimated their own home was worth in 2021, not a market price. Political lean is computed from Elections Canada's 45th General Election results, with each place located inside its riding. Drive times are routed on real roads.

    What is missing

    Indian reserves are not in this list. 34 were caught by the population filter and removed. They are real communities, but they are not places you can decide to move to, so ranking them as options would be both wrong and disrespectful.

    Residents have been researched for 71 of 712 places, marked in the last column. The rest are scored on measured data only and their column says so. Where a number is missing it reads .. and is dropped from the score rather than guessed at. The fit is a weighted power mean, not a plain average, so a place cannot fail the thing you care most about and win anyway by being mediocre everywhere else. Nothing here is a recommendation. It is a way to argue with a list.
