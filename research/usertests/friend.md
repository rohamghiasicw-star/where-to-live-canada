# Friend usability test — "Where to live in Canada"

Tester: 55, Ontario, not techy. On my phone. A buddy of mine built the thing I dreamed up in the gym change room and sent me the link. Let's see if it actually works.

---

## 1. Landing / first impressions

OK. Big black headline, "Where to live in Canada." Under it: "Every best-places-to-live list picks the weights for you. This one asks what *you* want, then re-ranks all 712 places in Canada against it."

Alright, I actually like that line. That's my whole idea in one sentence. 712 places is a big number, good, that means the small towns are probably in there, not just the same ten big cities.

Then there's a black line and a section "What you want — Set each one, and how much it matters."

First thing is "Climate" then "Winter — The January you want to wake up in." There's a slider sitting around the middle, and it says **-5°** off to the right. Hmm. So the slider is the temperature I *want*? Minus five. That's not obvious right away. I had to look at the little words under it: "Deep freeze" on the left, "No winter" on the right. OK so drag right for warmer. Fine, I get it now, but the -5 just sitting there confused me for a second — is that where I am or where I want to be? I *think* it's what I want.

Under the slider there are four buttons: Skip / A little / Matters / A lot. "Matters" is filled in black. So it already decided Winter "Matters" for me. That's a decent guess honestly, but nobody told me what these buttons do yet. I'm assuming it's how much I care.

Then "Summer — And the July." Slider at 20°, and it's set to "A little." Then "Snow — How much shovelling…" cut off at the bottom.

At the very bottom there are three tabs: **Answer / List / Map**. Answer is bold so I guess that's where the result is.

Honestly? Not bad. It's cleaner than I expected. But I don't fully understand the -5 number and I don't know what happens when I mess with the little Skip/Matters buttons. Let me just start doing what I came to do.
