# Usability test — "Where to live in Canada"

**Tester persona:** Margaret, 63, about to retire, Calgary. Comfortable with email/Facebook/online
banking but gets flustered by busy screens and small text. On an iPad/phone.
**Goal:** find a warm-ish, small, affordable town near a real city (hospital/airport for the
grandkids). Doesn't care about politics or nightlife. Wanted to turn OFF things she doesn't care
about (Skip).
**Site:** https://rohamghiasicw-star.github.io/where-to-live-canada/ (shows a "STAGING · work in
progress, may be rough" banner).
**Tested at:** phone 375x812 and tablet 768x1024. A wider desktop table was never reachable in the
browser pane, which matters (see below).

---

## First impression (thinking aloud)

- The big black title "Where to live in Canada" is lovely and clear. I know I'm in the right place.
- Then my heart sinks a little. There's a paragraph, then numbered steps, then a grey block of
  numbers and dates: "712 places... where 1,000 people live at real density... Toronto down to
  Annapolis Royal, pop. 530... Climate 1981-2010 · Smoke 2013-2024 · Census 2021 · Votes 2025."
  I don't know what "1,000 people live at real density" means and I don't know why I need the dates.
  It feels like homework before I've started.
- Good sign: a bottom bar with "Answer / List / Map" — that looks like where my results will be.
- Then "What you want" and a long, long list of things to set. I can already tell there's a lot here.

## Doing the task

### Winter (the thing I care about most) — I got stuck
- I wanted a milder winter, so I tried to drag the slider toward "No winter." Nothing moved.
- I tried tapping the bar where I wanted it (near "No winter"). Nothing moved. Still "-5°."
- I tapped right on the little handle. Still nothing.
- This is the single most frustrating moment. The ONE thing I care most about — how cold the winter
  is — sits behind a skinny slider I could not move by tapping or dragging on a touch screen.
  Tapping the track doesn't jump it either. A real me would be stuck here and probably give up.
- (The four-button bar under it — Skip / A little / Matters / A lot — DID respond to taps. So it's
  specifically the drag-slider that beat me.)

### The "how much it matters" bar — I mostly get it, but it's a lot
- Skip / A little / Matters / A lot. The intro explained it, and it makes sense: how much should
  this count. Fine as an idea.
- BUT every single item has TWO things to decide: what I want (top) AND how much it matters
  (the bar). Sixteen items. That's ~32 decisions. For someone who dislikes clutter this is a wall.
  Nothing tells me "it's okay to leave most of these alone."

### Turning things OFF (Skip) — works, but the "off" look is faint
- I set Summer to "Skip" (I don't care about it). It worked once I tapped it squarely.
- But "Skip" when selected is a faint grey, while "Matters/A lot" go bold black. I had to look twice
  to be sure my Skip had "taken." A nervous user won't trust it.
- Confusing overlap: Politics shows BOTH "Not fussed" (top row) AND "Skip" (bottom bar) lit up.
  What's the difference between "Not fussed" and "Skip"? They both feel like "I don't care." Two
  different ways to say the same thing, on the same item, is muddling.
- Nice: Politics is already "Not fussed / Skip" by default, so the thing I care least about is off
  without me touching it.

### Budget — another slider I couldn't move
- Same problem as Winter. "$600k" with $200k–$1.4M. I want cheap, but I couldn't drag it down.
  My second-most-important thing (affordable, fixed income) is also locked behind a slider I can't
  work by touch.

### The recommendation — didn't feel like it was for me
- It gave me "You should live in Woodstock, ON. It gets you the drive and the winter. You give up
  the sun." Woodstock Ontario is not warm. The list had Woodstock and St. Thomas at "Jan -5.8°" /
  "Jan -4.7°." I'm from Calgary trying to escape cold — why is it sending me to cold Ontario towns?
  (Because I could never move the winter slider warmer. But a real me wouldn't know that; I'd just
  think "this thing doesn't understand me.")
- The answer also changes live while I poke at things, and at one point the top changed to "Someone
  sent you their answers, so this is their result." I have no idea how I did that or how to undo it.
  Very disorienting.

### Reading a place's details — THIS part is good
- I opened St. Thomas, ON. Clean card: big name, tidy sections "Climate / The place / What residents
  say." Readable.
- I understood most numbers: "Snow 119 cm/yr," "Days below -20: 4" (useful to a cold-hater!),
  "Home, average value $470k," "Rent and utilities $940/mo," and best of all "Nearest city over
  300k: London 34min" — exactly what I care about for a hospital and airport.
- The little month-by-month temperature bar chart shows warm summer / cold winter at a glance. Nice.
- Two puzzlers: "Unemployment .. %" — the ".." looks broken/like a typo (it means "no data," but I'd
  never guess). And "Lean: leans right / Riding: Elgin--St. Thomas--London South" — I don't know
  what a "Riding" is and I don't care about politics.
- Honest tone I liked: "Not researched. Nobody has read what residents say about St. Thomas yet, so
  this column is empty rather than guessed at." That builds trust.

### The List and Map
- LIST (on the phone) is friendly: cards with a name, a plain sentence ("Gets you the drive and the
  winter"), a green score ("80"), and a few chips (Drive 50m, Jan -5.8°, People 47k). Much nicer
  than a spreadsheet. "670 of 712 clear your dealbreakers · show all" — I roughly get it.
- MAP is pretty but not useful to me — a spray of green dots over Canada I can't act on; some labels
  overlap (Bridgewater/Chester sit on top of each other). Legend "Fits you / Does not" is clear.

## Cross-device notes
- On phone AND tablet it's the same single tall column. The intro tells me to "Read the table for
  why, and hover any column heading to see what it means." There is NO table and NO hovering on a
  touch device — that instruction is written for a desktop I never see. So words like "Fit"/"Lean"/
  "Carfree" as column headings never actually appeared for me to hover.
- The "STAGING" banner blinks in and out and shoves the whole page down, so buttons jump around
  under my finger.
- Text size is fine-to-good; the headings and the detail card are nicely large. The grey small print
  (intro stats, methodology) is where it gets small and dense.

## Could I complete my goal on my own?
Only halfway. I could get an answer, open a town, and understand most of its numbers. But I could
NOT tell it my two most important things — "I want a milder winter" and "keep it affordable" —
because both are drag-sliders I couldn't move by tapping or dragging. So the town it gave me was
cold Ontario, the opposite of what I wanted. Without someone beside me to work those sliders, I'd
walk away thinking the site pointed me somewhere cold and wrong, and give up on the part that
matters most.

## Top 5 things that confused / overwhelmed me (ranked)
1. **The sliders won't move by touch.** Winter (how warm) and Housing budget (how cheap) — my two
   biggest priorities — are drag-only sliders. Dragging, tapping the handle, and tapping the track
   all did nothing. I got stuck on the most important controls.
2. **Two decisions per row × 16 rows.** A "what I want" control PLUS a Skip/A-little/Matters/A-lot
   bar for every one of sixteen items. ~32 choices, no signal that it's fine to skip most.
3. **"Not fussed" vs "Skip" — same item, both lit.** Two different ways to say "I don't care" on
   the same preference. I can't tell what the difference is or which one really turns it off.
4. **The result didn't match me, and it shifts under me.** Kept recommending cold Ontario towns
   (Jan ≈ -5°) to someone who wants warm; the answer changes live while I touch things; and it
   suddenly said "this is THEIR result" (a shared-link state I triggered by accident).
5. **Jargon + broken-looking bits + desktop-only instructions.** "1,000 people at real density,"
   "power mean," "Riding," "leans right," "µg/m³," and "Unemployment .. %" (the ".." looks like an
   error). Plus "read the table / hover any column heading," which doesn't exist on my tablet.

## 2 things that felt friendly / clear
1. **The place detail card.** Big readable name, tidy sections, plain labels with units, and exactly
   the fact I needed — "Nearest city over 300k: London 34min," plus "Days below -20: 4." Trustworthy
   and easy to read.
2. **The List cards + the honest tone.** Name, one plain sentence of "why," a green score, a few
   chips — friendlier than any table. And "empty rather than guessed at" made me trust the numbers.

## Fixes that would help someone like me
- Let me TAP the slider track to set it, or replace Winter/Budget sliders with big preset buttons
  (e.g., "Mild / Average / Cold"; "Under $400k / $400-700k / More"). Sliders are the wrong control
  on a touch screen for an older user.
- Make "Skip" the obvious off switch (bolder when chosen), and drop the redundant "Not fussed" so
  there's one clear way to turn something off.
- Make the answer feel intentional, not live-twitchy; give a clear "Start over" and don't drop me
  into a "someone sent you this" state without explaining it.
- Trim the opening stats/dates and the "read the table / hover" line on phone/tablet — I never see
  a table.
- Replace ".." with "no data" in plain words.
